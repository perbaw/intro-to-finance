# Deloitte Touche Tohmatsu Limited 
- one of the Big Four accounting organizations
- largest professional services network in the world
- founded in 1845, expanded to the US in 1890
- provides audits, consulting, financial advisory, risk advisory, as well as tax and legal services
- [Deloitte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deloitte)
# Ernst & Young
- one of the Big Four accounting firms
- headquartered in London
- primarily provides assurance (financial audits), tax, consulting, and advisory services to more than 150 countries
- [EY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernst_%26_Young)
# Facebook
- American online social media and social networking service 
- based in Menlo Park, California  
- over 2.5 billion monthly users today
- available in 111 languages
- [Facebook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facebook)
# Honda Motor Company Ltd. 
- Japanese public multinational conglomerate corporation 
- primarily known for its manufacturing of automobiles, motorcycles, and power equipment
- world’s largest motorcycle manufacturing company 
- world’s largest internal combustion engines manufacturer measured by volume
- [Honda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honda)
# KPMG International Cooperative
- one of the Big Four accounting firms
- found in 147 countries with over 219,000 employees 
- KPMG stands for “Klynveld Peat Marwick Goerdeler” due to the merger of KMG (Klynveld Main Goerdeler) with Peat Marwick in 1987 
- [KPMG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KPMG)
# MasterCard Incorporated
- American multinational financial corporation 
- processing payments between banks, and card-issuing banks or credit unions, for the purchasers of the MasterCard brand 
- publicly-traded company since 2006 
- about $12.5 billion in revenue (2017)
- [MasterCard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mastercard)
# McDonald’s
- founded in 1940 from a small restaurant operation based in San Bernardino, California
- transformed into a franchise that used the golden arches logo in 1953
- world’s largest restaurant chain by revenue
- [McDonald's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonald%27s)
# Merrill Lynch
- American investing and wealth management division of Bank of America
- responsible for over $2.3 trillion in client financial assets 
- over 14,000 financial advisors
- acquired by Bank of America in 2008
- rebranded as “Merrill” in 2009
- [Merrill Lynch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merrill_\(company\))
# Microsoft Corporation
- one of the pioneers of personal computer systems dating back to the mid-1980s
- responsible for software, consumer electronics, Microsoft Windows operating systems, Microsoft Suite, and Internet Explorer
- [Microsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft)
# Morgan Stanley
- American multinational investment bank and financial services company 
- founded in 1935 
- headquartered in New York City
- over 60,000 employees
- found in more than 42 countries.
- [Morgan Stanley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morgan_Stanley)
# NASDAQ Stock Market
- American stock exchange
- ranked second on the list of stock exchanges by market capitalization behind the New York Stock exchange
- initially an acronym for the National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations
- [NASDAQ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nasdaq)
# New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) 
- American stock exchange 
- located on Wall Street in Manhattan, NYC.
- world’s largest stock exchange by market capitalization 
- average trading value of around $169 billion (2013)
- its earliest associations can be traced back as far as 1792
- [NYSE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange)
# PricewaterhouseCoopers (PWC)
- one of the Big Four accounting firms
- based in London, United Kingdom
- second-largest professional services firm network
- operations in 157 countries 
- [PWC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PricewaterhouseCoopers)
# Target

- began as a division of Dayton’s Company of Minneapolis, Minnesota, in 1962
- began expanding nationwide in the 1980s

# Tesla Incorporated
- American electric vehicle and clean energy company 
- founded in 2003
- based in Palo Alto, California

# The Coca-Cola Company
- began as a product for patient medicine in the late 19th century
- named after its two original primary ingredients: coca leaves and kola nuts
- Coca-Cola has refined its original formula and has vertically and horizontally integrated to become one of the largest multinational beverage corporations
- [The Coca-Cola Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company)

# Volkswagen AG

- has maintained the largest market share in Europe for the past two decades
- [Volkswagen AG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volkswagen_Group)
